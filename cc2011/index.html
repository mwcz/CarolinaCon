CarolinaCon 7 Has Ended
Saturday, 14 May 2011 02:32

The seventh CarolinaCon has come and gone, but the hangover lingers on. In every way the Con was a huge success and an event for the ages. The staff would like to thank all of the awesome presenters, the side event coordinators, the hotel, t;The Hotel Bar , and the record crowd of attendees. Love and shout-outs to you all! The staff even wants to thank itself for a job well done.

We would also like to recognize the following CarolinaCon 7 contest winners:

  • Joel Kerr (49th Security Division) - G. Mark's CarolinaCon Crypto Challenge winner
  • Chris Truncer (guesswho) - Capture The Flag winner
  • G. Mark Hardy - Hacker Trivia round #1 winner
  • tokachu - Hacker Trivia round #2 winner
You guys are the 31337est of the 31337!

We look forward to seeing everyone again at CarolinaCon 8 in 2012. Keep Calm and Hack On!

CarolinaCon Seven - Updates
Monday, 24 January 2011 14:17

It's been a while since the last update but the CarolinaCon staff is running along at full speed and we've got a lot to tell you about.

In case you missed it, here is the link for getting the special $69/night room rate for the conference at the Holiday Inn on Glenwood Avenue in Raleigh, NC.

We have a very special surprise that all of our Twitter Followers found out about early. We have selected the first round of speakers for CarolinaCon Seven. The speakers and their presentations are:

  • Deral Heiland - If you own a Multi Function Printer (MFP), then I own you
  • Chris Teodorski - Kippos and bits and bits and bits: Fun with SSH honeypotting

CarolinaCon 7 Speakers Selected
Thursday, 10 March 2011 19:44

Well, the time has arrived for us to unveil the list of great speakers for CarolinaCon Seven. It was a very tough series of decisions this year to select the following speakers out of the plethora of talk submissions that we received.

In many cases, there were only a few staff votes separating those who were chosen with those who were not. We invite those speakers who were not chosen to still attend the conference and we are very grateful that they took time to send in a submission.

  • sec0ps - The Failure that is Penetration Testing
  • Gerry Brunelle - Dissecting the Hack: Malware Analysis 101
  • G. Mark Hardy - Tales from the Crypto
  • Deral Heiland - If you Own a Multi-Function Printer then I Own You
  • Chris Teodorski - Fun with SSH Honeypotting
  • Lisa Lorenzin - Security Lessons from Cracking Enigma
  • Thomas Holt - Do Personality Traits Increase the Likelihood that You will Hack?
  • Dr. Tran - How to Own and Protect Your Office Space
  • Omar Santos - Current and Future Trends in Cybercrime and Exploitation
  • mjg - Yara and Python: The Malware Detection Dynamic Duo
  • Branson Matheson - TTL of a Penetration
  • Nick Fury - Serial Killers: USB as an Attack Vector
  • Justin Troutman - Mackerel: A Progressive School of Cryptographic Thought
  • Ryan Linn - PIG: Finding Truffles without Leaving a Trace
  • purehate - Why your Password Policy Sucks
  • ronin - Hack from a Library with Katana
  • Adam Drew - Music and Audio Production with FOSS
  • Jordan Sissel - logstash: Open Source Log and Event Management
Also, if your talk was not chosen, please keep an eye on your inbox. In the event that any of the submitters has scheduling changes between the time of their submission and now, we will ask runners-up if they will graciously step in with their submission(s).

CarolinaCon 7 - April 29th, 30th, and May 1st, 2011!
Wednesday, 23 March 2011 14:34

For all of you out there in hacker land who are just now finding out about CarolinaCon, we wanted to put all of the relevant information right on the home page in one easy to find spot.

  • CarolinaCon Seven will be held on April 29th, 30th, and May 1st, 2011
  • CarolinaCon Seven will be held at the Holiday Inn on Glenwood Avenue in Raleigh, NC
  • Registration opens at 5:30pm on Friday, April 29th
  • Registration opens at 9:30am on Saturday April 30th
  • Registration will be open during general conference hours throughout the weekend
  • The total cost for CarolinaCon Seven is $20 (cash only) at the door
  • You cannot pre-register for CarolinaCon Seven
  • The schedule of talks and talk abstracts can be found ON THIS PAGE!

But wait, there's more! CarolinaCon is going to have a mixture of side events, including:

  • TOOOL Lockpicking Village
  • Capture The Flag (CTF)
  • Hacker Trivia
  • Hacker Movie Screenings
  • The Unofficial CarolinaCon Shoot
  • ....and more!!!
Keep an eye on this space for any last minute updates. We'll be seeing you at CarolinaCon Seven!
